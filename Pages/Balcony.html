Giving your balcony an eco upgrade

Balconies are one of the most versatile parts of your home, there are many different ways you can change it up. From low cost greenhouse to fully transformed green spaces, look below for all the ideas!

Growing Plants

  • Living Walls

    • Using wall space to grow plants instantly spruces up your balcony. Growing not leafy plants but fruits can provide an instant attraction for local insects! So instead of a plain brick wall consider:

    • Heuchera, Wallflowers and Violas for shaded areas
    • Petunia, Salvias and Fuschias for sun facing walls
    • Strawberries, Tomatoes and Herbs for something a little more edible

  • Greenhouses

    • Greenhouses are a very large part of every garden. They can still be made us of in smaller areas such as balconies. Consider using a larger thin greenhouses to sit against walls, or small glass cabinets to fit tied against the railing. Every nook and cranny can be converted into a thriving green space!

Introducing Sustainability in Green Spaces

  • Water Collecting Butts

    • Water rescources are under pressure from climate change. Rainwater can be collected from the roofs of homes, garages, greenhouses and other garden structures. Local councils and DIY stores are good places to buy water butts, but maintenaince of them is key. These sites below can help you find more information.

    • RHS - Water: collecting, storing and re-using
    • Rain Harvesting Systems - The legality behind collecting water

  • Composting

    • Composting is a vital part of any gardern's home, the versitality and simplicity of it makes it easy to be introduced anytime. You can start by just collecting food scraps and adding more and more materials as you go! Composting takes patience as it usually read in 6-24 months. However at the end you will end up with nutrious soil for your plants; a complete eco-friendly cycle!

    • RHS - Guide to composting
